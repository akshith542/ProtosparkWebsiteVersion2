Global Chapter Model

One Chapter. Every Country.

ProtoSpark scales internationally through a chapter system — one chapter per country, each led by a local Chapter Leader who bridges language, time zone, and cultural gaps to bring coding education to their community.

// How Chapters Work

01
Local Chapter Leader

A volunteer in each country runs their chapter, recruits local students, and coordinates with the global ProtoSpark team on scheduling and logistics.

02
Language Bridging

Chapter leaders translate instructions and check-ins into their local language. Core curriculum materials are prepared before camp begins to support leaders fully.

03
Time Zone Management

Sessions are recorded and posted for asynchronous viewing. Live sessions are scheduled to reach the most students globally; chapter leaders run supplemental local sessions where needed.

04
Centralized Coordination

All chapters coordinate via shared tools (Discord, Google Workspace). Bi-weekly standups on Tuesdays and Thursdays keep the global team aligned and supported.

Chapter Leader Requirements

Leadership roles are open to teens and young adults worldwide who want to make a real impact.

  • ✓ Recruit at least 3 students from your country or region
  • ✓ Bilingual: English + your local language
  • ✓ Basic knowledge of Scratch (or genuine willingness to learn before camp)
  • ✓ Attend bi-weekly standups (Tuesdays & Thursdays, 9 PM ET)
  • ✓ Passionate about making CS education accessible to every child
