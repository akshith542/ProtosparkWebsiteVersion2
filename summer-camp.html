Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the camp really free?

Yes — 100% free for all students. ProtoSpark is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit and our mission is to remove every barrier to CS education.

What ages can participate?

Children ages 6–13. Students are placed in age-appropriate groups (6–8, 9–11, and 12–13) so the pace and content is right for them.

Does my child need any coding experience?

Not at all! ProtoSpark is designed for beginners. Day 1 starts with the absolute basics of Scratch and we build from there.

What does my child need to participate?

A device (computer, tablet, or Chromebook) with internet access, Zoom installed, and a free Scratch account at scratch.mit.edu. No paid software needed.

What timezone are the sessions in?

Live sessions are scheduled to accommodate the largest number of global participants. All sessions are recorded for asynchronous viewing, and we'll share exact times when you register.

What if my child doesn't speak English?

We are actively working to translate curriculum materials into multiple languages. When registering, please note your preferred language in the timing/questions field and we'll do our best to accommodate.

What is the capstone project?

The capstone is a student-made interactive Scratch project — a game, story, or animation — that students build during Days 3–5 and present on the final day of camp. It's the highlight of the whole experience!

How do I contact ProtoSpark?

Email us at contact@protospark.org — we typically respond within 1–2 business days.
